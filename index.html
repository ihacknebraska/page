The mission

Grow cyber talent and strengthen local resilience.

Too many students encounter cybersecurity only after they have already chosen a path. iHackNebraska brings the field closer to classrooms, clubs, libraries, and local meetups.

The goal is simple: create more on-ramps, more mentors, and more useful projects so Nebraska grows talent from within.

How it works

A simple path from curiosity to contribution.

  1. Discover

    Short sessions introduce security basics, digital citizenship, troubleshooting, and everyday technology concepts.

  2. Build

    Teams use technology to solve practical problems, create useful projects, and volunteer their skills for schools, non-profits, and local partners.

  3. Connect

    Participants meet mentors, employers, educators, and organizations looking for motivated technical help.

Offerings

Choose the right entry point.

For students and career builders

Hands-on cybersecurity education

Focused sessions that explain core concepts, introduce useful tools, and give learners something concrete to finish.

  • Security fundamentals, Linux, networking, web safety, and cloud basics.
  • Small labs that fit a classroom, meetup, or weekend workshop.
  • Next-step guidance for resumes, portfolios, certifications, and internships.
For teams that learn by doing

Clubs, labs, and team challenges

Reusable formats for schools, libraries, and youth groups that want a sustainable technology club.

  • Meeting agendas, project prompts, and facilitator notes.
  • Team exercises for communication, troubleshooting, and systems thinking.
  • Low-cost activities that work with modest equipment and mixed skill levels.
For small businesses and non-profits

Digital resilience and readiness support

Approachable help for organizations that need better habits, clearer checklists, and a trusted place to ask questions.

  • Staff-friendly sessions for account safety, phishing, devices, and data handling.
  • Checklists for backups, updates, access control, vendor tools, and recovery planning.
  • Referrals to regional resources when an issue needs professional support.
For builders, helpers, and organizers

Meetups, clubs, and service projects

Ways to plug in as a mentor, facilitator, sponsor, project lead, or volunteer contributor.

  • Discord coordination for events, clubs, project teams, and mentor matching.
  • Service projects for schools, civic groups, and non-profits.
  • Organizer support for people who want to host or facilitate locally.
