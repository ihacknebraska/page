The mission

Grow cyber talent and strengthen local resilience.

iHackNebraska brings together students, professionals, businesses, educators, and technology enthusiasts to learn by doing. The initiative creates a collaborative environment where mentorship, community education, and practical skill-building can thrive.

By combining education, community outreach, after-school programs, and practical workshops, iHackNebraska aims to help Nebraska become a regional leader in cybersecurity talent and readiness.

How it works

Training, mentorship, and real-world practice.

  1. Learn

    Beginner-friendly and advanced workshops covering security fundamentals, digital citizenship, troubleshooting, and practical technology skills.

  2. Practice

    Community labs, team challenges, project nights, and guided practice sessions that help people learn together.

  3. Serve

    Projects and readiness support for local organizations that need practical help navigating emerging threats.

Offerings

Go deeper with practical cyber programs.

For students and career builders

Hands-on cybersecurity education

Beginner-to-advanced workshops that turn curiosity into working skill through guided labs, tool practice, and mentor-led learning.

  • Security fundamentals, Linux, networking, web safety, and cloud basics.
  • Project-based labs that build confidence without requiring prior experience.
  • Mentor pathways for students, career changers, and early professionals.
For teams that learn by doing

Clubs, labs, and team challenges

Structured learning environments for teamwork, problem solving, systems thinking, and digital resilience.

  • After-school club kits for schools, libraries, and community groups.
  • Scenario-based exercises for teamwork, troubleshooting, and communication.
  • Safe practice spaces for experimentation, curiosity, and guided learning.
For small businesses and non-profits

Digital resilience and readiness support

Practical guidance that helps local organizations understand risk, harden basics, and prepare for common threats.

  • Security awareness sessions for staff and leadership.
  • Readiness checklists for identity, backups, devices, email, and cloud tools.
  • Connections to trusted volunteers, mentors, and regional cyber resources.
For Nebraska's hacker and maker network

Meetups, clubs, and service projects

A collaborative space for learning together, building tools, helping community partners, and growing Nebraska's technology culture.

  • Discord coordination for events, clubs, project teams, and mentor matching.
  • Volunteer technical projects for schools, civic groups, and non-profits.
  • Community learning discussions that keep curiosity practical, welcoming, and safe.
